GROUNDING TOOLKIT - When Anxiety Spikes or Cravings Hit

30 seconds: Physiological sigh - double inhale through nose, long exhale through mouth. Fastest cortisol drop available.

5 minutes: Huff - Box Breathing (4-4-4-4). Parasympathetic activation. Use before hard conversations, after triggering emails, before sleep.

10 minutes: Huff - Wim Hof (3 rounds). Full nervous system reset. Floods the body with natural adrenaline then deep calm on the breath hold.

20 minutes: NSDR (Huberman protocol). Equivalent to 2 hours of sleep. Use after PT, after stressful work blocks, or when you feel yourself reaching.

Any time: Get near a window or step outside if you can. Natural light helps. Not a dealbreaker - the breathwork and NSDR do the heavy lifting.

WATER: 100oz DAILY

Non-negotiable. Speeds THC clearance (fat-soluble, stored in cells). Reduces withdrawal headaches and brain fog. Supports tissue healing from surgery. Track it or it won't happen.

SHAME VS. GUILT - Know the Difference

Guilt: "I did bad things." Guilt is useful. It points at behavior you can change.

Shame: "I am a bad person." Shame is a lie. It locks you in a loop: I'm not worth it, so I self-destruct, which proves I'm not worth it.

You are not a bad man. You are a good man who ran bad patterns. Patterns change. Identity doesn't have to be a prison. That's what these 8 days are for.

When the shame voice speaks this week, name it: "That's shame. That's not me."

Medications - Dr. Buckley's Orders

Aspirin 325mg

Twice per day with food. 30 days (through March 28). Prevents blood clots. Take at least 2 hours before Naproxen - Naproxen can block Aspirin's antiplatelet effect if taken simultaneously.

Colace (Docusate Sodium)

1 pill morning, 1 pill evening. Prevents constipation from oxycodone and anesthesia. Drink plenty of clear liquids. Keep prune juice on hand. If no bowel movement in 3-4 days, add Milk of Magnesia or Miralax.

Tylenol (Acetaminophen) - First Line

1,000mg (2 extra-strength tablets) every 6-8 hours as needed. DO NOT exceed 4,000mg in 24 hours.

Oxycodone - AS NEEDED Only

Narcotic. Use only when Tylenol isn't enough. Goal: transition off within 2-3 days to Tylenol only. No driving. No alcohol.

Naproxen 500mg

Twice per day with food for 10 days (through March 8). Prevents bony overgrowth (heterotopic ossification) and reduces inflammation. If GI upset, stop and increase icing and motion. DO NOT take ibuprofen, Motrin, Advil, Aleve, Celebrex, or Meloxicam while on this.

THE RULES

Protein at every meal. Vegetables at dinner 5-7 nights. 16oz water before every meal.

Eating Tips

Protein First

You're rebuilding tissue from surgery. Calorie needs are lower (you're not moving much). Protein needs are HIGHER. Target 20-30g protein at breakfast, 25-35g at lunch. Palm-sized, 1 inch thick at dinner. Hit protein before you think about anything else on the plate.

Vegetables Every Night

This is the one change that fixes the most problems. Fiber delays gastric emptying, prevents the blood sugar crash, kills the 10pm cravings, and supports blood pressure. Air fryer broccoli (12-15 min at 400), microwave frozen vegetables (5 min), or raw cucumbers and tomatoes (2 min). Three methods. No excuses. Pick one.

Plate rule: Half vegetables. Quarter protein. Quarter starch. One fist of starch, not two.

Water

16oz before each meal + 16oz between meals = ~80oz/day. Keep a full bottle next to your recovery spot at all times. Hydration directly affects tissue healing, joint recovery, and blood pressure regulation.

The 9pm Problem

Carb-heavy dinner without fiber = blood sugar spike at 7pm, crash at 9pm, hungry at 10pm. Vegetables at dinner fix this mechanically. If still hungry after 9pm: 16oz water, wait 15 min. Still hungry? Protein or fat, not carbs. If this happens every night, you didn't eat enough vegetables at dinner.

Boredom Eating

You're home all day recovering. Boredom mimics hunger. Before you open the fridge: "Am I hungry or am I bored?" Protein or apple sounds good = hungry. Eat. Chips or something indulgent = bored. Audiobook. Meditation. Anything but the kitchen.

Sodium - Blood Pressure

Watch the salt. Canned soups, deli meats, and processed foods are sodium bombs. Your blood pressure is already spiking. High sodium + Naproxen (which retains fluid) is a bad combination. Read labels. Aim under 2,000mg sodium per day. When the Naproxen course ends March 8, one BP aggravator goes away automatically.

The appetite fix is not willpower. It's fiber.

Vegetables at dinner. Water before meals. Protein at every meal. Do these three things and the 10pm cravings solve themselves.

Blood Pressure Notes

Three of your supplements actively support lower BP: Arg-Cit (vasodilation via nitric oxide, ~5/3 mmHg reduction), Mag Glycinate (natural calcium channel blocker, ~2-6/2-3 mmHg), and Omega-3 (mild BP reduction). These are working FOR you. Keep taking them.

Naproxen works AGAINST you on BP. NSAIDs cause sodium and fluid retention and can raise BP by 5-6 mmHg. This is temporary - Naproxen ends March 8. Monitor your BP and expect improvement after it stops.

If you end up on BP medication: flag the Arg-Cit with your doctor. Combined with BP meds it could push too low. At current supplemental dose this is low risk, but worth mentioning.
