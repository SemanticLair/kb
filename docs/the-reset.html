The Reset

RECOVERY PROTOCOL · FEBRUARY 2026

"Four to six weeks of recovery is either a cocoon or a coffin for your habits. You decide which."

Recovery Protocol

Four phases. One structure. Follow it.

THE HARD LINE

No THC. The entire reset collapses without this.

No work email or Slack until March 9. Tell Sunil.

Phone stays out of the bedroom.

One meditation per day is the floor, not the ceiling.

Kim is your accountability partner, not your audience.

Phase 1
Surrender
Days 1-4 · Thursday through Sunday
Goal: Accept help. Feel the pain without numbing it. Let the fog pass.

You just came out of surgery. Your only job is to let your body do its work. Do not fight the fog. Do not optimize the recovery. Do not check Slack "just to see." Receive.

No THC. The post-op meds handle the pain. THC on top of that doesn't manage pain - it manages feelings. That's exactly what you're trying to stop doing. Let the discomfort exist. It passes.

Supplements back online. Full AM stack at 6am, PM stack at 9:30pm. Confirm timing around post-op meds with your surgeon. The stack is your first act of self-care each day. Don't skip it.

One meditation per day. Even 10 minutes. You're lying down anyway. Huberman's NSDR protocol or Yoga Nidra on Insight Timer. This is not about enlightenment. It's about training your nervous system to settle without a substance.

Let Kim run everything. The house. The kids. The meals. The ice machine schedule. You will feel the pull to manage it. That pull is the same impulse that has you running 12-hour days and absorbing everyone else's work. Name it. Let it go. Receive help without directing how it's given.

"The reset costs more."

When you reach for the phone, the THC, the work email - the cost of abandoning this reset is higher than the discomfort of staying in it. Every time.

Phase 2
Clearing
Days 5-9 · Monday through Friday
Goal: Build the daily structure. Survive the cravings. Start feeling again.

The fog lifts. The pain settles into something manageable. And the THC cravings arrive, because your brain is looking for the off switch it's been using for months. This is the hardest phase. It's also where everything changes.

Wake Up

Breathwork before you touch your phone. Box breathing (4 count in, 4 hold, 4 out, 4 hold) or Huberman's physiological sigh (double inhale nose, long exhale mouth). Five minutes minimum. This replaces the old pattern of waking up and immediately reaching for stimulation.

Midday

Guided meditation. 15 minutes minimum. Waking Up app (Sam Harris) is built for your brain - structured, intellectual, no woo. Or browse Insight Timer. This is the anchor of your day. Everything else can flex. This doesn't.

Afternoon

Journal. One prompt. One page max. Not therapy. Not freeform rambling. One specific question answered honestly. The daily anchor question: "What am I avoiding feeling right now?" Phase-specific prompts are in the Journal section.

Evening

Yoga Nidra or body scan before sleep. This directly replaces the THC sleep crutch. Ally Boothroyd on YouTube or Insight Timer. Earbuds in, lights off. No screens after 9pm. Audio only.

When Ready

Foundation Training. Ask your surgeon when you can start gentle decompression breathing, even seated. This isn't about fitness. It's about inhabiting your body again instead of escaping it. You know these movements. Your body remembers.

Phase 3
Rebuild
Days 10-13 · Leading into WFH
Goal: Deepen the habits. Define who you're becoming. Let Kim in.

You made it through the cravings. You have a daily structure. You're feeling things you haven't felt in months - some of it good, some of it hard. That's the point. Now build on the foundation.

Stack meditation to 20+ minutes. You built the habit. Now go deeper. Longer sits reveal what shorter ones hide.

Morning walk if cleared. Even short. Even on crutches. Morning sunlight in the first hour after waking resets your circadian rhythm and gives you a natural dopamine hit that doesn't cost you anything.

Identity statements. Sit down and write three completions of this sentence: "I am a man who ___." Not goals. Not aspirations. Identity declarations. Present tense. Write them on a card. Put the card where you'll see it every morning.

The conversation with Kim. You owe her one honest conversation about what the last six months have been like inside your head. Not a confession. Not an apology marathon. Not an explanation designed to earn credit. Just truth: what happened, how you got lost, what you're doing about it. Then stop talking and listen to what she says back. That part matters more.

Phase 4
Hold the Line
March 9-21 · WFH Weeks
Goal: Maintain every habit while work pressure returns. Set one boundary.

This is where most resets die. The laptop opens. Slack pings. Help desk tickets pile up. The familiar current pulls you back into 12-hour days and reactive mode. Every habit from Phase 3 continues. No exceptions. No "just this once."

Morning routine completes before the laptop opens. Supplements. Breathwork. 10-minute meditation. In that order. Every single day. The laptop stays closed until all three are done. This is the firewall between the man you're becoming and the pattern you're leaving.

Set one work boundary. Not five. One. Pick the one that matters most and hold it for two weeks straight. "I am not available after 6pm." Or "I am not picking up help desk tickets during WFH recovery." Or "I am not answering ad-hoc executive requests same-day." One boundary, held consistently, teaches you more than ten boundaries written down and broken.

Keep journaling. Even two sentences. "What did I do today that was for me, not for performance?" is enough. The volume doesn't matter. The daily practice of self-honesty does.

Watch for the Nice Guy pattern. You will feel the pull to prove you're still indispensable while working from home. You'll want to over-deliver to justify the recovery time. That impulse is the old pattern wearing a new costume. Name it. Let it pass. You earned this time. You don't need to earn it again.

"Compound interest on performance."

Every day you hold the line, the habits get stronger. Every day you skip, you're paying interest on the restart. The math is simple. Trust it.

Journal Prompts

One prompt per day. The daily anchor never changes.

Daily Anchor: "What am I avoiding feeling right now?"

Phase 1 (Days 1-4)

What did I believe back surgery would fix that it did not?
When did I decide I was not worth fighting for? What was the moment?
What does Kim see when she looks at me right now? What do I wish she saw?
If pain is a message, what is my hip trying to tell me?

Phase 2 (Days 5-9)

What am I using THC to avoid feeling? Name it specifically.
Where in my life am I performing competence instead of living it?
What would I do differently at work if I was not afraid of being seen as less?
What does Ryan need from me that I am not giving? What about Hailey?
When was the last time I let someone help me without managing how they did it?

Phase 3 (Days 10-13)

Complete this three times: "I am a man who ___."
What boundary have I never set that I know I need to?
What would my life look like in 12 months if I held every habit I built this week?
What do I want to say to Kim that I have not said?

WFH Weeks (March 9-21)

What was the first work impulse I felt this morning? Did I act on it or breathe through it?
Am I absorbing work that should belong to someone else? Name it.
What did I do today that was FOR me, not for performance?
Did I hold my boundary today? If not, what story did I tell myself to justify breaking it?

The Truth

You survived back surgery. Your body healed. You showed up.

The hip was always a separate problem that needed its own fix. It was not a failure of the first surgery. It was not a betrayal by your body. It was a second thing that needed attention, and you had the courage to address it even after the first recovery knocked you down.

"I don't give a shit" is armor, not truth. A man who doesn't care doesn't build a recovery protocol. Doesn't ask what to listen to. Doesn't worry about squandering the time. You care so much it terrifies you. The armor is there because last time you cared and hoped, the hip kept hurting and it felt like your body lied to you.

The THC is not the problem. It is what a good man reaches for when he decides the pain of feeling is worse than the cost of disappearing. But disappearing has a cost too. Kim sees it. Ryan and Hailey feel it. And somewhere under the fog, you feel the distance growing and it makes everything worse.

This surgery is not a magic reset. The labrum repair gives you a body that works. The protocol gives you a structure to follow. But the actual turnaround - the one you're hoping for - that comes from one decision: to believe it might work without requiring a guarantee first.

You don't need to know it will work. You just need to be willing to find out.

"The reset costs more." When tempted.

"Compound interest on performance." When executing.

You have the structure. Follow it. Trust it.
